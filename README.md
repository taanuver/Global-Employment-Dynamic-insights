# 🌍 Global Employment Dynamic Insights

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes global labor market trends from 1991 to 2025, covering 183 countries. It focuses on employment, unemployment, gender disparities, and the impact of major global events such as COVID-19.

The analysis is performed using Python, with data visualization techniques to uncover meaningful insights for decision-making.

---

## 📁 Dataset Details
- Countries: 183  
- Time Period: 1991–2025  
- Features:
  - Country
  - Gender (Male, Female, Total)
  - Age Groups
  - Year
  - Employment Rate
  - Unemployment Rate  

---

## 📈 Key Insights
- Employment rates show a gradual decline over time with a sharp dip during COVID-19 (2020)
- Youth (15–24) unemployment is significantly higher than adults
- Gender gap persists, with male employment consistently higher than female
- Strong inverse relationship between employment and unemployment

## 💡 Business Insights
- High youth unemployment indicates need for skill development programs
- Gender gap highlights untapped workforce potential
- Post-COVID recovery trends show opportunities for economic policy planning
- Countries with stable employment trends are better investment targets
